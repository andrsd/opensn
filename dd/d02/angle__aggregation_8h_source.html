14/**Angle aggregation has to cater for running the 8 corners of a 3D
15 * partitioning, the 4 corners of a 2D partitioning (the latter 2 both being
16 * polar angle aggregation) as well as single angle aggregation.
17 *
18 * At the most fundamental level this manifests as a number of angle indices
19 * that share a SPDS, however SPDS do not have to be unique which allows
20 * the notion of polar angle sets.
21 * For single angle aggregation each SPDS is associated
22 * with a single angle index. For polar angle aggregation a single SPDS can
23 * have multiple indices associated with the same azimuthal angle but
24 * different polar angles. We call this manifestation a "AngleSet".
25 *
